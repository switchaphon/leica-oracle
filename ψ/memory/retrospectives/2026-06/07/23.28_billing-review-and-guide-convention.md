## Honest Feedback

**Friction 1: Convention blindness.** I built the billing-payment page without reading call-to-opd or open-quick-view first. This is the same mistake pattern as not checking DS before creating new components — I know the rule exists (it's in my memory) but I don't consistently apply it when the task feels straightforward. A "simple wrapper page" doesn't feel like it needs research, but that's exactly when conventions matter most.

**Friction 2: Minimalist bias on guide steps.** I defaulted to 1 step because it seemed "enough" — the billing flow starts with one click. But the convention is clearly to annotate every interactive touchpoint in the flow, not just the entry point. The guide is for someone who has never seen the prototype, not for me who just built it.

**Friction 3: Un had to correct me twice in sequence.** First the path collision, then the single-step guide. Both could have been avoided by spending 2 minutes reading sibling pages. Instead Un had to spend energy explaining something that was already documented in the codebase. This wastes their time and erodes trust.

## Lessons Learned

1. **Study every sibling before creating new pages** — read ALL files in the same directory, not just the closest match. 2 minutes of reading saves 20 minutes of corrections.
2. **Activity flow guide convention**: each interactive element gets a data-guide attribute + a PrototypeGuide step. Not just the entry point — the full journey.
3. **Activity flow sub-routes must have unique paths** — never reuse the base page path. Follow `{base}/{activity-slug}/page.tsx` pattern.
