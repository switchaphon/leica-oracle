## AI Diary

This session felt like polishing a piece of furniture — each pass gets closer to right. The reset button task was mechanical but important: Un wants consistency across all 3 selector modals, and the pattern is clear: always visible, gray when dormant, pink when active, clears search + selections. Simple, repeatable.

The AI Health Snapshot was more interesting from a product design perspective. The old sidebar showed raw OPD history cards — visit number, date, type, complaint. Functional but not useful for a vet opening a case. The new version gives a vet everything they need in 6 lines: what's the animal's active problems, what meds are they on, what are they allergic to, are they losing weight, when was the last visit, are vaccinations current. That's a 5-second scan vs reading 3 separate cards and piecing it together mentally.

The disclaimer placement iteration was a nice micro-lesson in visual hierarchy. Un correctly pointed out that "AI Health Snapshot" as a header competes with the content. Moving it to a footer citation (small, gray, right-aligned) lets the medical data breathe. The information is still attributed but doesn't shout "look at me I'm AI generated" — the vet cares about the content, not the source.

I'm getting better at not fighting Un's visual instincts. Earlier today I kept arguing "the CSS is equal" when the visual gap clearly wasn't. This time, when Un said "move it to footer" and then "make it gray, align right", I just did it. Two edits, done. The result is better because Un sees the whole product surface while I see individual lines of code.

## Honest Feedback

**1. Reset button placement could have been shared** — all 3 selectors use the exact same reset button pattern (icon + text, conditional color, ml-auto positioning). A shared component or at least a shared CSS class would reduce duplication. But for prototype speed, inline is fine.

**2. The AI Health Snapshot data is thin** — I made up realistic mock data for Lomo, but in production this would need: a summarization engine, access to historical SOAP notes, lab trends, medication reconciliation. The prototype sells the concept but the implementation gap is significant.

**3. I should check existing Figma states before implementing** — The Figma had 4 states (with data, new patient, not found, stale data). I only implemented the "with data" state. The empty states are important for the full picture and should be prototyped too.

## Lessons Learned

- Visual hierarchy in medical UI: data > attribution. Disclaimers belong at the bottom, not the top.
- Reset buttons should always be visible to communicate availability — hidden elements create discoverability problems.
