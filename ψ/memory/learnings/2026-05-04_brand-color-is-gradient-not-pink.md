Pixel insight during pink-vs-red collision review: the POPS brand is not "pink" — it's a gradient (pink → orange → yellow). All three colors are brand-legitimate. Orange (#F07A00) is the logo's "intersection" point where two shapes connect, making it a strong semantic match for interactive primary actions.

This means there's a path to split: pink for identity surfaces (sidebar, gradient, brand moments), orange for CTA buttons, red for destructive — using all three brand colors with semantic intention rather than overloading pink for both identity and interaction.

Validated by: Neon's perceptual analysis (pink #E5007D and red #DC2626 are only 32° apart in hue, collapse under protanopia), and Pixel's reference to how medical systems (Epic, Meditech) keep brand in shell and use neutral/specific colors for interaction.
